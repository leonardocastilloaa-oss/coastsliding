What We Pay Attention to in Fort Lauderdale

Door movement tells the story

A door that drags from the first inch usually points to roller wear or panel height. A door that catches in one place often points to track damage or debris.

Security depends on alignment

Many locks fail because the panel settled, not because the lock suddenly broke. We check both before recommending replacement.

Drainage matters in Florida rain

Blocked weep holes can push water toward the interior floor. Track cleaning is part of a complete local repair.

Glass and windows need context

Fogging, cracks, failed balances and worn cranks each point to different repairs. The goal is targeted service, not unnecessary replacement.
