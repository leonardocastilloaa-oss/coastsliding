Questions Answered

Real answers to what South Florida homeowners actually search for.

How much does sliding door repair cost in South Florida?+
Most repairs range from a modest service call to a few hundred dollars depending on the issue. We provide a flat written quote before starting — no surprise fees. Getting multiple repairs in one visit saves on the call fee.
Why do sliding doors fail faster in South Florida?+
South Florida's salt air, humidity, UV intensity and daily patio use corrode standard steel rollers faster than inland. We use marine-grade stainless steel on coastal repairs.
Do I need to replace my entire sliding door?+
Almost never. In most cases, what fails is the hardware — rollers, tracks, locks, seals — not the frame or glass. We repair first whenever practical.
What cities do you serve?+
We cover Miami-Dade, Broward and Palm Beach counties from Homestead to Jupiter, including Miami, Fort Lauderdale, Boca Raton, West Palm Beach, Hollywood, Coral Springs and dozens more.
How quickly can you come?+
In Miami-Dade and Broward, we typically arrive within 2–6 hours of your call on weekdays. Palm Beach is usually next-day. Many jobs are completed in one visit.
Do I need a permit?+
For most repair work — rollers, tracks, locks, like-for-like glass — no permit is required. Impact glass replacement may need NOA documentation. Full unit replacement requires a permit, which we can help coordinate.
Is there a warranty?+
Yes. Roller replacements carry a lifetime warranty on parts. Other repairs include workmanship warranty terms based on the repair type.
Do you speak Spanish?+
Yes — estimates, explanations and invoices can be handled in English or Spanish.
